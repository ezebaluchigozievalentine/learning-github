Rich and Flavorful Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is one of Nigeria’s most beloved dishes, known for its bold flavors and hearty texture. Made from ground melon seeds (egusi), this soup is thickened with palm oil and often enriched with leafy greens, meat, and fish. The result is a richly seasoned stew that's both nourishing and deeply satisfying—perfect with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.

To create this culinary masterpiece, egusi seeds are ground and fried into a paste before being simmered with meat stock, palm oil, onions, and assorted proteins like beef, goat meat, and dried fish. Bitterleaf or spinach is often added for a fresh kick, while native spices and seasoning cubes bring out a signature taste that warms the soul.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 500g assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe)
  • 200g dried fish or stockfish
  • 1 cup chopped spinach or bitterleaf
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 2 cups meat stock or water

Steps

  1. Season and boil the assorted meats until tender, then set aside with the meat stock.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat and sauté the chopped onions until translucent.
  3. Add the ground egusi and fry for 5–7 minutes, stirring continuously to form a thick paste.
  4. Pour in the meat stock and stir gently to loosen the egusi paste into the liquid.
  5. Add the boiled meats, dried fish, crayfish, seasoning cubes, and pepper. Simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  6. Mix in the chopped spinach or bitterleaf and cook for another 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.
